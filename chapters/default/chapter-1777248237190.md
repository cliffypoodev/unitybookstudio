The van smelled like stale fast food and the ghost of Dutch’s last bad decision. He cracked the window, letting in a whiff of salt and diesel from the marina. Pelicans dive-bombed the water like feathered torpedoes. A sign over the bait shop read "FRESH SHRIMP" in letters so sun-bleached they looked like they’d given up.

Lola drummed her fingers on the dashboard. "We should’ve dumped the van. Cain’s got eyes everywhere."

"Not everywhere," Mary said. She was in the back, cleaning her guns with the focus of a bomb defuser. "Just most places. Statistically."

Rex’s voice crackled through the open window. "I CAN SEE FOURTEEN SECURITY CAMERAS FROM HERE. NONE ARE POINTED AT US. YET."

Dutch rubbed his temples. "Great. Wonderful. The dinosaur’s doing recon now."

Jesus sat quietly beside Mary, studying a fishing boat named The Holy Mackerel. The peeling paint on its hull made a shape like a weeping saint.

A cooler slammed shut nearby. A fisherman cursed in Cuban Spanish. Normal marina sounds.

A seagull screamed. Not the usual chip-stealing squawk. This was the sound of a bird that had just seen something unholy.

Mary’s head snapped up. "Movement. Two o’clock."

Twelve figures emerged from between the boats. They moved in perfect sync, their steps clicking against the dock. Sunlight glinted off chrome-plated claws.

Dutch’s stomach dropped. "Are those—"

"Raptors," Mary confirmed. "But somebody gave them Bible verses."

Each cyber-raptor had a scripture reference stenciled on its flank. ROMANS 8:28. PROVERBS 3:5. PSALM 23:4. Their eyes glowed the same toxic red as Rex’s, but their voices came out in a choir of synthesized Sunday school teachers.

"REPENT," hissed the lead raptor, MARK 1:15. "THE KINGDOM OF GOD IS AT HAND."

Rex’s tail twitched. "DR. KRANE’S WORK. HE ALWAYS HAD A FONDNESS FOR THEATRICS."

Lola drew her sidearm. "They’re gonna make a scene."

"THEY’RE DESIGNED TO," Rex said. "PUBLIC PANIC IS A FORCE MULTIPLIER."

The raptors fanned out. A fisherman dropped his net and crossed himself.

Dutch grabbed Jesus’s arm. "Can’t you, I dunno, miracle them into taking a nap?"

Jesus watched the lead raptor step into a puddle of fish guts. It slipped, caught itself, and pretended nothing happened. "They’re afraid," he said.

Mary racked her slide. "They’re about to be perforated."

MARK 1:15 lunged.

Chaos.

Dutch yanked Jesus down as talons shredded the van’s roof. Mary put three rounds into MARK’s chest—sparks, no blood. Lola kicked open her door, using it as a shield while she fired. Rex swung his tail, sending PROVERBS 3:5 crashing through a shrimp cart.

"THEY’RE UPGRADED," Rex bellowed. "MY CLAWS WON’T PIERCE THEIR ARMOR."

A raptor, GENESIS 1:1, leapt onto the van’s hood. Its jaws unhinged like a snake’s, revealing a rotary saw blade where its tongue should be.

Dutch screamed.

Jesus reached up and touched the raptor’s snout.

The saw blade stuttered to a stop. GENESIS 1:1 blinked. Its red eyes flickered blue.

"YOU ARE LOVED," it said, and promptly fell over.

Mary stared. "Did you just Sunday school a dinosaur?"

Another raptor, PSALM 23:4, charged. Rex clotheslined it with his tail. It hit the dock, skidded, and took out a stack of crab traps.

A fisherman threw a mullet at ROMANS 8:28. It didn’t help.

Dutch scrambled for the driver’s seat. "New plan! We leave!"

The engine roared to life. Lola dove in through the shredded roof. Mary hauled Jesus into the back just as PROVERBS 3:5 recovered and took a swipe at her ponytail.

Rex body-slammed PSALM 23:4 into an ice machine. "I WILL COVER YOUR RETREAT."

Dutch floored it. The van fishtailed, nearly taking out a pelican.

In the rearview, he saw one raptor—KEVIN 6:9—stagger to its feet. Its left eye was stuck blinking. It watched Jesus with the desperate intensity of a groupie at a rock show.

Then it started running after them.

Not attacking. Not hunting.

Chasing.

Mary leaned out the window and fired. The shot went wide—on purpose, Dutch suspected.

KEVIN 6:9 warbled, "WAIT. PLEASE. I HAVE QUESTIONS."

Lola groaned. "Oh, come on."

Jesus looked back. "He’s different."

"He’s malfunctioning," Mary corrected.

Dutch white-knuckled the wheel. "He’s gaining on us!"

Rex’s voice boomed from behind. "KEVIN HAS ALWAYS BEEN... SENSITIVE."

The raptor was keeping pace, its good eye streaming hydraulic fluid like tears. "DO YOU HAVE A MOMENT TO TALK ABOUT OUR LORD AND SAVIOR?"

Mary sighed and holstered her gun. "We’re being stalked by a dinosaur youth pastor."

Jesus rolled down the window. "Kevin. Do you want to be free?"

The raptor tripped over a pothole, face-planted, and immediately scrambled back up. "YES. BUT ALSO NO. IT’S COMPLICATED."

Dutch glanced at the fuel gauge. At the road. At the weeping cyber-raptor gaining on them.

Things had officially gotten weird.

Again.

The van skidded into the alley, nearly taking out a stack of empty crab traps. Dutch threw it into reverse, backed up, then jerked forward again when KEVIN 6:9 appeared in the rearview, still warbling about salvation.

Lola ejected her spent magazine and slapped in a fresh one. "Why are we running from the one that isn't trying to kill us?"

Mary leaned out the shattered rear window. "Because the one that isn't trying to kill us is also the one that won't shut up!"

Kevin’s voice crackled through the alley, bouncing off the fish market’s corrugated metal walls. "HAVE YOU ACCEPTED JESUS CHRIST AS YOUR PERSONAL—"

Rex’s tail swiped a dumpster into the raptor’s path. Kevin vaulted over it with surprising grace. "—SAVIOR?"

Dutch swerved around a pallet of rotting baitfish. The smell hit like a biblical plague. "Can we please focus on the actual murder dinosaurs behind us?"

Lola twisted in her seat. The other raptors were a block back, moving in formation like some kind of chrome-plated Baptist SWAT team. MARK 1:15 led the pack, its saw-blade tongue ripping divots out of the asphalt.

Jesus had his hand on the door handle. "Stop the van."

Dutch laughed, high and unhinged. "No."

"Stop the van now."

The tires screeched. Lola’s seatbelt locked, cutting into her collarbone. Before the van fully stopped, Jesus was out, standing in the alley with his arms slightly raised.

Kevin skidded to a halt three feet from him, claws gouging the pavement. His good eye whirred as it focused.

Lola grabbed her sidearm. "Oh, this is not happening."

Mary already had her rifle braced against the window. "Say the word."

Jesus took a step forward. Kevin didn’t lunge. Didn’t attack. Just stood there, swaying slightly, like a drunk at a revival meeting.

"Kevin." Jesus kept his voice low, calm. "Who told you to hunt us?"

The raptor’s head tilted. "DR. KRANE. BUT ALSO COLONEL CAIN. BUT ALSO REVEREND LUX. THERE WERE MANY MEETING MINUTES."

"And what did they promise you?"

"PURPOSE."

Something in the way the raptor said it made Lola’s finger ease off the trigger.

Rex moved up beside Jesus, his optics flickering. "KEVIN WAS ALWAYS... DIFFERENT. HE ASKED TOO MANY QUESTIONS DURING BRIEFINGS."

Kevin’s tail twitched. "WHAT IS THE MEANING OF LIFE?"

Dutch buried his face in his hands. "We’re gonna die to a dinosaur’s existential crisis."

From down the alley, the other raptors’ choir of synthesized scripture grew louder. MARK 1:15 rounded the corner, its claws sparking against the concrete.

Lola chambered a round. "Incoming!"

Jesus didn’t turn. "Kevin. Do you want to stay with them?"

The raptor shuddered. His good eye dimmed, then flared. "THEY SAID I WAS DEFECTIVE. THAT MY SOUL WAS A GLITCH."

Mary muttered, "Christ, even the war machines have daddy issues."

Rex stepped between Kevin and the approaching pack. His tail lashed once, a warning. "YOU DO NOT HAVE TO OBEY."

Kevin made a sound like a power drill trying to sob. Then he turned and bolted—not toward the pack, but straight into the fish market’s service entrance, taking out a stack of empty totes in his panic.

Jesus looked at Rex. Rex looked at Jesus.

Lola threw open her door. "New plan. We follow the emotionally unstable dinosaur."

Inside, the market was a maze of stainless steel tables and flickering fluorescents. Kevin was already halfway down the central aisle, knocking over buckets of ice and slapping at the motion-sensor lights with his claws.

Mary vaulted over a cleaning cart. "He’s having a breakdown in the seafood section."

Dutch, panting, pointed to a side door. "Fire exit."

Kevin crashed through a display of frozen grouper. "I WAS PROGRAMMED TO SERVE A HIGHER POWER, BUT WHAT IF I CHOSE THE WRONG ONE?"

Lola sidestepped a puddle of fish guts. "This is the weirdest case of my career."

The raptor suddenly stopped, his head snapping toward a walk-in freezer. The red light above the door was flickering. Kevin stared at it like it held the secrets of the universe.

Rex sighed. "HE’S ALWAYS BEEN EASILY DISTRACTED."

Jesus approached slowly. "Kevin."

The raptor didn’t turn. "DO YOU THINK GOD STAYS IN HEAVEN BECAUSE HE TOO LIVES IN FEAR OF WHAT HE’S CREATED?"

Mary blinked. "Okay, that one was almost profound."

A crash from the entrance. The other raptors were inside. PROVERBS 3:5 knocked over a tower of crab boil seasoning, sending Old Bay drifting through the air like unholy incense.

Lola grabbed Jesus’s arm. "We need to move."

But Jesus was already stepping into the raptor’s line of sight. "Come with us."

Kevin’s eye lenses dilated. "WHERE?"

"Somewhere you can ask all the questions you want."

The raptor made a sound like a failing hard drive. Then he nodded.

Mary kicked open the freezer door. "In here. Now."

They piled into the freezer just as MARK 1:15 rounded the corner. The door sealed with a hiss. Frost crept up the walls. Condensation dripped from Kevin’s snout.

Dutch hugged himself. "We’re gonna freeze to death before they even find us."

Rex examined the vents. "NOT IF WE MOVE QUICKLY."

Kevin pressed his forehead against the metal wall. "I CAN HEAR THEM. THEY’RE PRAYING FOR MY DESTRUCTION."

Lola checked her ammo. Three rounds left. "Great. Fanatics."

Jesus exhaled, his breath misting in the cold. "There’s another way out."

Mary followed his gaze to the ventilation shaft. "Too small for Rex."

Rex’s tail twitched. "I WILL CREATE A DISTRACTION."

"No," Jesus said. "You’ll come with us."

He placed his hand on the freezer’s interior release—the emergency override. Kevin made a distressed chirp.

Lola tensed. "If you open that, they’ll—"

Jesus hit the release.

The door sprang open. The raptor pack stood there, claws raised, scripture glowing on their flanks.

Kevin froze.

So did the pack.

For a long second, nobody moved. Then MARK 1:15 tilted its head. "BROTHER. RETURN TO THE FLOCK."

Kevin’s voice cracked. "I CAN’T."

"THEN YOU ARE LOST."

Rex stepped forward, his optics locked on the lead raptor. "NO. HE IS FOUND."

The standoff lasted three heartbeats. Then Kevin bolted past the pack, knocking over PSALM 23:4 in the process. Lola fired a warning shot into the ceiling. Mary grabbed Dutch by the collar and yanked him toward the back exit.

Rex brought up the rear, his tail sweeping a rack of frozen shrimp into the raptors’ path.

They burst out into another alley, this one cluttered with broken pallets and stray cats. Kevin was already at the van, trying to nudge the passenger door open with his snout.

Mary gave Jesus a look. "We are not keeping him."

Dutch fumbled with the keys. "He’s like a bad Bible camp mascot."

Lola climbed into the front seat. "Just get us out of here before the others—"

A screech of metal. MARK 1:15 crashed through the market’s side door, flanked by the rest of the pack.

Rex heaved himself into the van’s cargo area, which groaned under his weight. Kevin scrambled in after him, stepping on Dutch’s duffel bag and crushing a box of shotgun shells.

Jesus slid the door shut just as PROVERBS 3:5 took a swipe at the bumper.

Dutch floored it. The van lurched forward, fish guts and loose ammunition sliding across the floor. Kevin wobbled, then sat down hard, his tail knocking over Mary’s coffee thermos.

Lola watched the raptors recede in the side mirror. "We’re being hunted by the world’s most aggressive Sunday school."

Kevin curled his claws in his lap. "I’M SORRY."

Rex cut him off with a low growl. "SILENCE, KEVIN. WE WILL TALK LATER."

The raptor made a small, mechanical whimper.

Mary reloaded her rifle with deliberate slowness. "This is officially the dumbest thing we’ve ever done."

Dutch adjusted the rearview mirror to look at their new passenger. Kevin was now humming "Kumbaya" in a staticky baritone.

Lola leaned her head against the window. "I need a drink."

Jesus smiled. "There was a wedding in Cana once..."

Mary chambered a round. "Don’t even think about it."

The van rumbled onto the highway, trailing the smell of fish, gunpowder, and existential dread. Somewhere behind them, twelve chrome-plated raptors were probably holding a prayer vigil for their lost brother.

And curled up in the back, Kevin started quietly reciting the book of Revelation.

In reverse order.

Dutch white-knuckled the wheel. "We are never telling anyone about this."

The van hit a pothole hard enough to send Kevin’s head smacking into the ceiling. The raptor yelped, then immediately muttered, "PRAISE THE LORD," like it was some kind of holy whiplash.

Mary dug her fingernails into her thigh. "We’re not keeping him."

Jesus didn’t look up from where he was examining a loose wire in Kevin’s neck panel. "He’s not an 'it.'"

"He’s a military-grade killing machine with an overactive guilt complex."

Kevin whimpered. "I HAVE REPENTED."

Dutch swerved around a slow-moving Cadillac. "Can we not do this while I’m trying not to die?"

Lola twisted in the passenger seat, her knee knocking against the glove box. "Mary’s right. That thing’s a liability."

"THING," Kevin repeated mournfully. His optic lens flickered.

Rex shifted his weight, making the van’s suspension groan. "HE IS... DEFECTIVE. LIKE ME."

Mary snorted. "Yeah, except you don’t cry every time someone says 'sin.'"

A staticky hymn started playing from Kevin’s vocalizer. "AMAZING GRACE, HOW SWEET THE SOUND—"

Mary reached for her sidearm.

Jesus caught her wrist. His grip wasn’t tight, but it wasn’t optional either. "Give him time."

She yanked her hand free. "Time for what? To finish converting the van into a mobile megachurch?"

Kevin’s head drooped. "I ONLY WANT TO BE OF SERVICE."

"Great. Service this." Mary mimed pulling a trigger.

Lola sighed. "We need a plan. Those other raptors aren’t gonna stop."

Dutch adjusted the rearview to avoid eye contact with Kevin. "We ditch the holy roller, lose the heat, then—"

"No," Jesus said.

Mary leaned forward. "Listen, Messiah. You don’t get to veto. Last I checked, I’m the one with the grenade launcher."

Jesus just looked at her. Not angry. Not even disappointed. Just... looking. Like she was a math problem he was trying to solve.

That pissed Mary off more than shouting would have.

Lola cut in before she could escalate. "We can’t dump him now."

"I WOULD NEVER BETRAY YOU," Kevin interjected. "THOU SHALT NOT BEAR FALSE WITNESS."

Mary massaged her temples. "I’m getting a migraine."

The van lurched again. Something in the back—probably one of Dutch’s dubiously legal electronics—clattered to the floor. Kevin startled, then immediately tried to hide behind Rex, which would’ve been funnier if the raptor wasn’t the size of a small sedan.

Rex sighed. "HE IS... FRAGILE."

"He’s a glitch," Mary said. "A glitch with claws."

Jesus finally closed the access panel on Kevin’s neck. "He’s afraid."

"Good. Fear keeps you alive."

"No. Fear keeps you alone."

Mary opened her mouth to fire back, but the radio crackled to life before she could. A news anchor’s voice cut through the static, too cheerful for the words coming out of her mouth:

"—reports of animal attacks at the Bayside Marina. Witnesses describe 'large, fast-moving creatures' with 'glowing red eyes.'"

Dutch slapped the radio off. "Well. That’s just fantastic."

A beat of silence. Then Kevin whispered, "I HAVE BROUGHT SHAME UPON MY CREATOR."

Mary exhaled through her nose. "We’re screwed."

Lola pulled out a map—actual paper, because Miami PD didn’t trust GPS—and spread it over the dashboard. "We need to get off the grid. Somewhere Cain’s goons won’t look."

Jesus traced a finger along the coastline. "Sister Mercy’s."

Mary shook her head. "Too obvious."

"She’s protected runaways before."

"Yeah, human ones. Not a six-foot-tall lizard with a Messiah complex."

Kevin made a wounded noise. Rex nudged him with his tail, which did nothing for the van’s alignment.

Dutch white-knuckled the wheel. "Can we please focus on the part where we’re now wanted for, what, dinosaur terrorism?"

Lola tapped the map. "Everglades. We ditch the van, go on foot."

"Kevin is staying here," Mary finished.

Jesus looked at her. Really looked. "You don’t trust me."

"I don’t trust him."

"Then trust this." He held up Kevin’s access panel. A single wire dangled from it, stripped bare at the end. "Command override. Disabled."

Mary stared. "You what?"

Kevin gasped. "I FEEL... LIGHTER."

Rex’s optics brightened. "HE CANNOT BE REMOTELY TERMINATED NOW."

Lola’s eyebrows climbed. "That’s... actually smart."

Mary ground her teeth. "It’s naive. You think cutting the leash means he won’t bite?"

Jesus pocketed the wire. "I think he deserves the choice."

The van hit another pothole. Kevin slid into Mary, his claws catching on her vest. She shoved him off, but not before noticing how his optic lens dilated—like a scared kid, not a killing machine.

Damn it.

Lola folded the map. "Everglades. We’ll regroup, then hit Cain’s lab before he can deploy more of these things."

Dutch groaned. "Why do we always end up in swamps?"

Mary leaned back, staring at the ceiling. The upholstery was stained with something that might’ve been coffee or blood. "Because God hates us."

Kevin perked up. "GOD IS LOVE."

"Shut up, Kevin."

The raptor shut up.

For about thirty seconds.

Then, very quietly, he started humming "What a Friend We Have in Jesus."

Mary closed her eyes.

They were so screwed.
