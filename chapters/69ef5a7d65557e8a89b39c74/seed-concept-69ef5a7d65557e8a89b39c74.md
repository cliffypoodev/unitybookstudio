# BOOK 3 STORY BIBLE PROMPT

# **Machine Gun Jesus: Book 3 — Sins of the Everglades**

You are helping write **Book 3** in the *Machine Gun Jesus / Neon Messiah Rex* series.

This book is an anthology-style sequel made of **10 connected short-ish stories** set after Book 2. The stories should work individually, but together they should build a larger arc about the Everglades becoming infected by leftover Eden World biotech, Resurrección drug residue, THORN-7 command fragments, and early traces of the Serpent Engine.

Book 1 introduced Jesus in 1980s Miami, REX-777, Project LAZARUS REX, Eden World, Dutch’s holy weed, Detective Lola Vega, Sister Mercy, Trigger Happy Mary, Reverend Bobby Lux, Colonel Cain, Dr. Elias Krane, La Reina Blanca, Kevin the cyber-raptor, and the Serpent Engine.

Book 2 dealt with the aftermath: Eden World debris spreading through Miami, THORN fragments, Lux’s fake miracle schemes, scattered Resurrección contamination, Judas-9’s quiet introduction, Cain’s command ghost returning through backup nodes, and the discovery of Unit 777-C.

Book 3 now moves the danger into the swamp.

The core idea:

**The Everglades are not just contaminated. They are beginning to remember.**

The swamp has absorbed Eden World runoff, dinosaur tissue, holy weed spores, Resurrección residue, THORN-7 signal fragments, Cain’s backup code, and temporal rift energy. Animals mutate. Plants become semi-sentient. old crimes resurface. cartel routes become haunted. drowned military experiments wake up. swamp churches start seeing miracles. and the Serpent Engine begins testing whether biology can be commanded the same way machines can.

This book should feel like:

**southern gothic swamp horror + 1980s Miami crime + Jurassic creature chaos + stoner screwball comedy + spiritual weirdness + buddy action.**

The tone should stay ridiculous, profane, violent, funny, and unexpectedly sincere.

# REQUIRED CONTINUITY FROM BOOKS 1–2

Use these facts consistently:

## Jesus / J.C.

Jesus is still trapped in 1980s America. He is calmer now, but increasingly disturbed by how quickly people turn miracles into products, weapons, scams, or cults.

He does not like being treated as a celebrity, product, mascot, or magic dispenser.

He can heal, reveal truth, calm animals, disrupt corrupted machines, and occasionally alter water/plants/weed into miraculous substances. His miracles should feel powerful but not convenient. They should create moral consequences.

In Book 3, Jesus should become more aware that the Everglades themselves are wounded. The swamp is not evil. It has been poisoned by human greed, military experiments, cartel dumping, and spiritual corruption.

Jesus’s Book 3 emotional question:

**Can creation be healed after humans teach it violence?**

## REX-777 / Rex

Rex is a half-cyborg, half-gene-spliced T-Rex who broke free from Project LAZARUS REX after Jesus awakened his conscience.

He is still huge, terrifying, partially mechanical, vulnerable to THORN command frequencies, and emotionally afraid that his free will is fragile.

He protects Jesus fiercely. He is attached to Bartholomew the duck. He is jealous of Kevin. He is confused by human rituals but increasingly curious about morality.

In Book 3, Rex should feel a pull from the swamp. Some of the new creatures recognize him as either king, father, monster, saint, or escaped prototype. He hates all of those labels.

Rex’s Book 3 emotional question:

**If I was the first escaped monster, am I responsible for the others?**

## Detective Lola Vega

Lola is still the law-enforcement anchor, though her career is badly compromised. She keeps unofficial files on the Holy Chomp incidents.

In Book 3, Lola investigates illegal dumping, missing persons, cartel routes, fake federal cleanup crews, and swamp disappearances.

She is practical, dry, tired, and still furious that reality now includes cyber-dinosaurs and prophetic ducks.

Lola’s Book 3 emotional question:

**What does justice look like in places the official system abandoned?**

## Dutch Donovan

Dutch is a stoner conspiracy burnout, bad businessman, accidental prophet, and keeper/abuser of the Burning Bush OG strain.

In Book 3, Dutch believes the swamp is “talking in bad vibes.” He is partly joking and partly right.

His prophecies should be weird, dumb, and occasionally useful.

Dutch’s Book 3 emotional question:

**Can he stop turning miracles into scams long enough to actually serve something bigger than himself?**

## Sister Mercy Malone

Sister Mercy is rebuilding her shelter network after the damage caused by Lux and Eden World fallout. She represents grounded, practical faith.

In Book 3, Mercy becomes the link to poor swamp communities, migrant workers, abandoned families, and off-grid people harmed by dumping and cartel routes.

Mercy’s Book 3 emotional question:

**Can mercy reach people who have been ignored so long they no longer trust rescue?**

## Trigger Happy Mary

Trigger Happy Mary is Mercy’s estranged sister, an over-armed bounty hunter chaos engine with a good heart and horrible impulse control.

She should be loud, hilarious, violent, surprisingly tender, and wrong in unexpected ways.

In Book 3, Mary thrives in the swamp far more than anyone expected. She has old contacts among gator poachers, moonshiners, fan-boat smugglers, fake cryptid hunters, and one mime she still owes money to.

Mary’s Book 3 emotional question:

**Can she protect people without turning every swamp problem into a fireworks accident?**

## Kevin

Kevin is the semi-redeemed cyber-raptor. He wants a purpose beyond command programming. He is anxious, strange, useful, and not fully trusted.

In Book 3, Kevin is physically better suited to the swamp than Rex but emotionally more vulnerable to THORN echoes.

Kevin’s Book 3 emotional question:

**Can a creature built for ambush learn to belong without hiding?**

## Judas-9

Judas-9 was quietly introduced in Book 2. He is an android designed to betray Jesus but is developing guilt and independent will.

In Book 3, he should appear only lightly, as a background helper, informant, or unsettling presence. He should not reveal everything yet. Plant seeds for Book 6.

## Reverend Bobby Lux

Lux is disgraced but still active. In Book 3, he should be less central than Book 2, but his people may exploit swamp miracles for broadcast content.

## La Reina Blanca

La Reina escaped Book 1 with biological samples and continues building a Resurrección empire.

In Book 3, she should become more important again. Her cartel is testing swamp-grown Resurrección variants, prehistoric drug plants, and obedience compounds.

## Colonel Cain

Cain is not physically present. He exists as command ghost, file, signal, backup node, or voice.

In Book 3, Cain’s voice appears in drowned military equipment, THORN fragments, old radio transmissions, or corrupted animal behavior.

## Dr. Elias Krane

Krane is presumed dead/missing, but his lab notes and creature designs are still active. In Book 3, his swamp research should be discovered.

## Bartholomew the Duck

Bartholomew remains Rex’s tiny emotional-support duck. Do not over-explain him. He may be weirdly prophetic, immune to contamination, or just a duck. Keep it ambiguous.

## Contamination Sources

Book 3 should use multiple types of contamination:

1. **Resurrección residue** — creates aggression, partial revival, hallucinations, and biological instability.

2. **Eden World runoff** — mutates animals/plants and carries temporal energy.

3. **THORN-7 command fragments** — cause obedience behavior, hive responses, and machine-like animal movement.

4. **Burning Bush OG spores** — create truth visions, spiritual awareness, and occasional accidental miracles.

5. **Serpent Engine signal** — corrupts systems of life toward hierarchy, control, and domination.

6. **Krane’s buried experiments** — old proto-creatures, abandoned embryo tanks, and coded gene drives.

## Swamp Communities

Do not make the Everglades only monsters. Include people:

migrant workers
fishermen
off-grid families
Seminole-adjacent communities should be handled respectfully and not turned into mystical clichés
gator hunters
airboat guides
poor churches
farm workers
drug runners
environmental scientists
retired veterans
hurricane-damaged trailer parks
children who know more about the swamp than adults

The heroes should protect these communities from being exploited by cartels, government cleanup crews, fake preachers, and biotech scavengers.

## The Swamp Hum

A recurring phenomenon in Book 3.

At night, certain areas of the Everglades emit a low-frequency hum that affects:

Rex’s implants
Kevin’s programming
Dutch’s dreams
Jesus’s wounds
animal behavior
old radios
Bartholomew’s silence

The hum is an early biological expression of the Serpent Engine.

Book 3 should end by confirming the hum is not random. It is a signal.

# BOOK 3 LARGER ARC

Book 3 begins with strange swamp incidents after Eden World’s collapse.

At first, each incident seems isolated:

a protective mutant gator
a drug-laced church revival
memory mosquitoes
a haunted houseboat
a carnivorous greenhouse
a prophetic frog
a swamp song

But the stories reveal a pattern:

The swamp is being converted into a **living command network**.

By the final story, the team discovers that the Serpent Engine is trying to leave machines and enter ecology. It wants a living network of plants, animals, water, insects, and infected humans capable of carrying command signals.

The final phrase of Book 3 should point forward:

**“The machine has learned to grow.”**

---

# BOOK 3 STRUCTURE

Write Book 3 as 10 connected stories.

Each story should include:

A strange swamp incident.
A clear crime, mystery, or threat.
A character-specific emotional beat.
A continuity clue feeding the larger arc.
A mix of horror, action, absurd comedy, and sincere spiritual stakes.
A final breadcrumb pointing toward the next story or the book-wide mystery.

# STORY 1

# **Gator Saint**

## Core Premise

A massive mutated alligator begins protecting migrant workers from traffickers and cartel enforcers in the swamp.

## Main Cast

Jesus
Rex
Lola
Sister Mercy
Mary
Dutch
Bartholomew
A migrant worker named Rosa
A trafficker named Calhoun Briggs
The mutated alligator, later nicknamed **Saint Chomp**

## Setup

Sister Mercy hears from a swamp clinic that workers are disappearing near a tomato transport route. Survivors claim a giant alligator with glowing scars saved some people and ate others.

Lola suspects traffickers. Mary suspects “weaponized swamp justice.” Rex suspects competition.

## Main Conflict

The alligator is not evil. It was exposed to Eden World runoff and Resurrección residue. It attacks traffickers but is becoming more aggressive and less able to distinguish threat from fear.

The traffickers want to capture it and sell it to La Reina’s people.

## Comedy

Mary tries to baptize the gator with a fire hose.
Dutch insists the gator is “basically Catholic now.”
Rex is jealous because locals start calling the gator “the swamp’s holy beast.”
Bartholomew rides on Rex’s head and stares down the gator.

## Emotional Beat

Jesus sees a wounded creature trying to protect the vulnerable but corrupted by human poison.

Rex sees himself in Saint Chomp: created by violence, trying to choose protection.

## Continuity Clue

Saint Chomp’s scars glow in circuit-like patterns. Lola finds a metal tag embedded in its hide:

**KRANE FIELD TRIAL — BIOCOMMAND WETLAND SERIES**

## Ending

Jesus calms Saint Chomp enough for Mercy’s allies to relocate it to a hidden sanctuary.

The final scene shows swamp grass bending in perfect rows after the gator leaves, as if obeying an unseen command.

# STORY 2

# **Swamp Communion**

## Core Premise

A backwoods revival church serves communion laced with Resurrección, causing parishioners to hallucinate dinosaurs, confess crimes, and briefly obey the preacher’s every command.

## Main Cast

Jesus
Lola
Dutch
Mary
Sister Mercy
Reverend Silas Pike
A young parishioner named June
La Reina’s chemist courier

## Setup

A revival video reaches Lux’s old broadcast circles: a swamp preacher appears to be performing mass miracles. People are collapsing, speaking in unknown languages, and seeing “the beasts of Eden.”

Sister Mercy asks Jesus to investigate before Lux exploits it.

## Main Conflict

The preacher is not fully aware of what he is doing. La Reina’s courier supplied him with altered communion wine as a test market for obedience drugs.

The drug combines Resurrección with watered-down Burning Bush residue.

## Comedy

Dutch accidentally becomes guest preacher while high.
Mary keeps trying to frisk the choir.
Jesus is deeply offended by grape drink being called wine.
Rex waits outside and is mistaken for part of the revival spectacle.

## Emotional Beat

Jesus confronts the difference between genuine spiritual hunger and chemical manipulation.

June, a young parishioner, admits she wanted the miracle to be real because her family is desperate.

## Continuity Clue

The communion drug causes all affected parishioners to chant the same phrase:

**“The root obeys the crown.”**

## Ending

The team stops the courier and exposes the drug. Pike breaks down, realizing he let desperation make him a tool.

Lola keeps a sample of the communion compound.

Kevin experiences Cain lab memories and realizes some of his memories are implanted.

## Continuity Clue

## Ending

The breeding pool is burned, but one vial of larvae is stolen by a Cain-coded cleanup drone.

**BIOLOGICAL COMMAND VIABLE.**

**THE MACHINE HAS LEARNED TO GROW.**

**“No. But they can choose not to become the wound.”**

Final image:

Deep under the black water, a single root wraps around a broken THORN fragment.

It pulses once.

Green.

# BOOK 3 WRITING RULES

Keep the stories connected but readable as individual episodes.

Do not over-explain the Serpent Engine yet.

Do not make the swamp evil. The swamp is wounded.

Do not make every creature a monster. Many are victims.

Keep Jesus morally serious but still capable of dry humor.

Keep Rex scary, funny, and emotionally sincere.

Keep Mary insane, but give her growth through restraint.

Keep Dutch funny, but make his mistakes cost something.

Keep Lola grounded in investigation.

Keep Sister Mercy focused on vulnerable people.

Keep Kevin weird, anxious, and redeemable.

Keep Bartholomew unexplained.

Every story should include at least one absurd image that feels impossible to forget.

Examples:

Rex refusing to enter a mosquito cloud.
Mary in a beekeeper suit with ammo belts.
Jesus exorcising a houseboat with jumper cables.
Dutch jealous of a prophetic frog.
Rex being spiritually challenged by a mutant gator.
A moonshine ark full of glowing raccoons.
A sinkhole full of humming bones.
A swamp singing through every radio in Miami.
A living root network made of vines, wires, bones, and broken crowns.

End Book 3 with dread, but not despair.

This series is insane, but underneath the insanity it is about mercy, free will, corruption, chosen identity, and broken things refusing to stay broken.
